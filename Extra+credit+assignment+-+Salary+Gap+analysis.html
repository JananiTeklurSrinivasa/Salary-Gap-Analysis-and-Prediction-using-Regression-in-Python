SALARY GAP ANALYSIS¶

The dataset was obtained from kaggle and focuses only on Data related jobs and the salaries all round the world in the years 2020-2024. Following are some of the columns that I will be focussing on for the analysis and attempt to derive some insights:

  • job_title
  • experience_level
  • employment_type
  • work_models
  • employee_residence
  • salary_in_usd
  • company_size

Aim of the Analysis¶

  • The analysis aims to find insights on the impact of several factors like experience level, company size, work models, job title etc on the salary.
  • In addition to this, I will also be trying to predict the salary based on other factors based on which ones correlate the most.

Questions that the analysis aims to answer¶

  • How do different work models affect salary? Does on-site workers get paid more?
  • What are the top highest paid job title?
  • Does company size affect the pay especially at different experience level?
  • Can we come up with a model to predict salary based on some the factors that correlate with the salary column?
  • For on-site workers, which area of residence offers the highest salary?

def standardize_title(x):
    if 'BI' in x or 'Visualization' in x or 'Business Intelligence' in x:
        return('BI developer')
    elif 'Data Engineer' in x or 'ETL' in x or 'Data DevOps Engineer' in x:
        return('Data Engineer')
    elif 'Data Scientist' in x or 'Data Science' in x:
        return('Data Scientist')
    elif 'AI' in x:
        return('AI Engineer')
    elif 'ML' in x or 'Machine' in x or 'Deep Learning' in x or 'NLP' in x:
        return('Machine Learning Engineer')
    elif 'Data Analyst' in x or 'Data Analytics' in x:
        return('Data Analyst')
    elif 'Data Architect' in x:
        return('Data Architect')
    elif 'Data Operations' in x:
        return('Data Operations')
    elif 'Management' in x or 'Manager' in x:
        return('Data Manager')
    elif 'Computer Vision' in x:
        return('Computer Vision Software Engineer')
    elif 'Research' in x:
        return('Research Engineer/Analyst/Scientist')
        
    else:
        return(x)
salary['std_job_title'] = salary['job_title'].apply(standardize_title)

C. Predictive Analysis using Regression¶

  • We see that the experience level and the company size somewhat impact the salary.
  • Let us use regression with these two variables as predictors to predict the salary

# QR decomposition of X
Q, R = np.linalg.qr(X)

def predict_salary(company_size, experience, work_model):
    theta = [54275.45261147   , 2309.79114552 ,38034.4848897 , 16298.32368613]  # [Intercept, theta1,2,3]  
    return theta[0] + theta[1] * company_size + theta[2] * experience + theta[3] * work_model

D. Conclusion¶

Following are the insights we derived by analysing the Data science jobs dataset:

  • Hybrid workers get paid lesser than Remote/Onsite workers with a gap% of 43.63%.
  • Job titles like ML Engineer, Data scientist, Architects earn more than a Data Quality technichians or Data analyst.
  • A medium sized company pays more for its employees at Entry-level, Mid-level and Senior levels and the salaries are pretty much the same for Executive levels across various company sizes
  • The salary for data jobs has been on the rise since the year 2020!
  • For On-site workers countries like Israel, Japan, New Zealand, United States offer the best pay!
  • A predictive model was developed using company size, experience level, and work model as input features. The model predicted a salary of $113,227.84 for a mid-level employee working remotely at a medium-sized company.
